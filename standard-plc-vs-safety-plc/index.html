AUTOMATION vs FUNCTIONAL SAFETY

Standard PLC vs Safety PLC

Although both controllers look similar and can control industrial machines, they are designed for different purposes. A Standard PLC automates the process, while a Safety PLC protects people, equipment, and the environment.

Controls the machine, executes normal logic for production, speed, sequence.

Safety PLC

Protects people & equipment, executes certified safety functions with diagnostics & redundancy.

If a Safety Function is RequiredUse a Safety PLC

A Safety PLC is designed and certified so that if certain internal faults occur, the safety function continues to operate correctly or the system automatically fails in a safe state.

INTERVIEW ANSWER

A Standard PLC is designed to automate and control industrial processes, while a Safety PLC is specifically designed to perform certified safety functions. A Safety PLC is built so that even if certain internal faults occur, it can continue to perform the safety function correctly or move the system into a safe state. This makes it suitable for applications such as Emergency Stops, safety gates, and light curtains where protecting people and equipment is critical.

INTERVIEW ANSWER

Safety Integrity Level (SIL) is a measure of how reliable a safety function is. A higher SIL means a lower probability of dangerous failure and is used in applications where the consequences of failure are more severe.

✓
  • SIL measures the reliability of a safety function — not the device alone.
  • SIL is not just a ranking — it reflects how unlikely a dangerous failure should be.
  • As SIL increases: reliability ↑, acceptable PFD ↓, requirements become more stringent.
  • Exact numbers aren't the focus — understanding that higher SIL = greater reliability is the key concept.
